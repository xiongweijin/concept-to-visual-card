# Visual Formats

Pick the smallest format that explains the concept clearly.

## Single-Page Knowledge Card

Best for: 朋友圈, 小红书, quick sharing, study notes.

Structure:
- Strong title
- One visual metaphor or simple diagram
- 3-5 short explanation blocks
- One takeaway sentence

Use when the concept can be explained without a full story.

## Parable Comic

Best for: philosophy, psychology, abstract theory, memorable teaching.

Structure:
- 4-8 small panels
- A concrete everyday story
- A final reversal or insight
- Optional bottom concept mapping

Use when the concept needs emotional memory or an indirect explanation.

## Storyboard

Best for: process, behavior change, user journey, product mechanism.

Structure:
- Sequential panels
- Clear cause and effect
- Minimal text per panel

Use when time order matters.

## Analogy Diagram

Best for: technical or academic ideas with a clean correspondence.

Structure:
- Left side: familiar scene
- Right side: concept structure
- Arrows or labels linking the two

Use when mapping is the main insight.

## Infographic Poster

Best for: frameworks, lists, decision trees, comparisons.

Structure:
- Header
- Dense but organized sections
- Icons, arrows, hierarchy

Use when the user needs scanning more than storytelling.

## Public Account Cover

Best for: WeChat article covers, blog posts, content series.

Structure:
- Large title
- One symbolic visual
- Very low text density

Use when click-through and recognition matter more than full explanation.

## Presentation Concept Slide

Best for: PPT, internal talks, thesis defense.

Structure:
- One headline
- One central diagram
- 2-3 supporting labels

Use when the image must work at a distance.
